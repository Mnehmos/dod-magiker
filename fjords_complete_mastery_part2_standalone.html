/// ALCHEMY ///
The Art of Matter and Transformation

Alchemy is control over the physical world‚Äînot living tissue, but the base matter of reality. Stone, metal, glass, air, water. Transmutation, manipulation, creation and destruction of physical substance. This is the Art that built the Philosopher's Stone, that turns lead to gold, that makes the impossible tangible.

Working with Alchemy taught me that matter has memory. Every substance "remembers" what it was, what it's been, what it could be. When you transmute something, you're not just changing its form‚Äîyou're rewriting its history.

/// CHORA ///
The Art of Space and Distance

Chora is the manipulation of space itself‚Äînot traveling through it, but bending, folding, extending, compressing it. Distances are suggestions. Locations are negotiable. The universe is a map that can be redrawn. This is the Art that makes teleportation possible, that creates pocket dimensions, that lets you touch something on the other side of the world.

Working with Chora taught me that space is not empty. It's full of... something. A substrate. When you bend space, you're pushing against that substrate. And sometimes, it pushes back.

/// CHRONOS ///
The Art of Time and Causality

Chronos is the most dangerous Art I know. Not because it's flashy like Pyr or violent like Vita‚Äîbecause it breaks causality. When you manipulate time, you're not just changing what happens; you're changing what HAPPENED, what WILL happen, what COULD have happened. Every Chronos working creates paradoxes that reality has to resolve, usually in ways that screw over the caster.

I've learned that time is not a river flowing one direction. It's more like an ocean‚Äîcurrents flowing every which way, eddies and whirlpools, tides pulling in contradictory directions. And when you dive in, you risk drowning in moments that haven't happened yet or already did or never will.

Use Chronos sparingly. Time holds grudges.
