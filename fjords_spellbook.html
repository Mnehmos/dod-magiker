FJORD'S GRIMOIRE

A Compendium of High Ritual Workings
For the Collection & Study of Reality Scars
— By Fjord the Hermit —
Practice: High Ritual
Author's Preface:

This grimoire documents my workings in the pursuit of Reality Scars—those precious tears in the fabric of consensus reality where the Man's control falters. Each spell herein has been tested and refined through countless hours of preparation. Remember: High Ritual is not for the impatient. We trade speed for precision, haste for power.

The Arts listed represent minimum competency. Your mileage may vary.

— F.T.H., written in the Sanctuary, Year of Our Discontent

/// Level One: Novitiate Workings ///
These are the foundations. Simple, reliable, essential. I cast these in my sleep—which, given my habit of sleepwalking during geometric meditation, is not entirely metaphorical.
Unseen Hand
EXPERTISE 1 UTILITY DR 12
The fundamental telekinetic working. Move small objects without physical contact—perfect for retrieving components from across the Sanctuary, opening locks from the other side, or impressing the credulous.
MY METHODOLOGY: I inscribe a temporary circle of chalk mixed with iron filings. The geometric precision must be exact—I use a compass and straightedge, naturally. While focusing on the object, I recite the Hermetic formula of extension in pseudo-Latin, visualizing silver threads connecting my will to the target.
⚙ Mechanics
Component Value Notes
Required Arts Pyr +1 To generate kinetic force
Base DR 12 Simple task
Duration Until dawn Or until concentration breaks
Weight Limit ~10 lbs Heavier objects +2 DR per 5 lbs
Prep Time d6 hours Standard High Ritual
Components: Chalk, iron filings, clear line of sight
Backlash Risk: Minor. Worst I've experienced was objects flying at my face.
The Fool's Curtain
EXPERTISE 1 MISDIRECTION DR 14
Creates a minor illusion or sensory misdirection. I use this constantly when collecting components in public spaces. Makes observers think they saw something mundane rather than a robed madman harvesting reality fragments.
MY METHODOLOGY: A mirror is essential—I use a small silver compact. I write what I want people to perceive on parchment in mirror-script, burn it while gazing into the reflection, and speak the words backwards. The smoke carries the false image into their perception.
⚙ Mechanics
Component Value Notes
Required Arts Pneuma +1 To affect minds/perception
Base DR 14 Standard magical difficulty
Witnesses +2 DR per witness Affects 1-3 people reliably
Duration d6 minutes Or until they examine closely
Components: Small mirror, parchment, match
Backlash Risk: Moderate. I've had my own perceptions scrambled before.
⚠ Warning:
Do NOT use on more than 5 people simultaneously unless you enjoy the sensation of your brain leaking out your ears. I learned this at a bus stop. Still have the headaches.
Hermit's Beacon
EXPERTISE 1 UTILITY DR 12
Creates a small, floating light that follows me around. Indispensable for exploring abandoned buildings, sewers, and other places where Reality Scars tend to manifest. Much better than fumbling with a flashlight, which inevitably triggers Surveillance State.
MY METHODOLOGY: I capture natural light in a glass vial during the day (preferably at exact noon for maximum potency). At night, I release it through a geometric focus—usually a quartz prism. The light "remembers" being free and follows the command to illuminate my path.
⚙ Mechanics
Component Value Notes
Required Arts Pyr +1 To manipulate light/energy
Base DR 12 Simple working
Duration Until dawn Light fades with sunrise
Brightness Candlelight 15-foot radius
Components: Glass vial, captured sunlight, quartz prism
Backlash Risk: Minimal. Worst case: temporary blindness.

/// Level Two: Adept Formulations ///
Here the real work begins. These spells require not just knowledge but understanding. The geometry becomes more complex, the timing more precise, the consequences of failure more severe.
The Walking Door
EXPERTISE 2 TRAVEL DR 18
Short-range teleportation through folded space. Essential for escaping danger or reaching Reality Scars in inaccessible locations. I've used this to bypass locked doors, scale walls, and once—memorably—to escape a pack of feral cyborgs in the ruins of a Tesla factory.
MY METHODOLOGY: This requires a doorframe. Any doorframe. I draw sigils on both sides with blessed chalk, then visualize the destination while walking through repeatedly (exactly seven times—six won't work, eight causes problems). On the seventh passage, I step through to my target location instead of the room beyond.
⚙ Mechanics
Component Value Notes
Required Arts Chora +2 To fold space
Base DR 14 Standard difficulty
Line of Sight +4 DR If destination not visible
Final DR 18 For blind teleport
Range ~100 feet Longer distances +2 DR per 100 ft
Prep Time d6 hours Includes creating doorframe focus
Components: Doorframe (portable frame works), blessed chalk, exact knowledge of destination
Backlash Risk: SEVERE. Telefragging is real. I once materialized halfway through a filing cabinet.
⚠ Critical Warning:
NEVER teleport to a location you cannot visualize clearly. I learned this when I tried to reach "the nearest Reality Scar" without specific coordinates. Spent three days in a place that was simultaneously nowhere and everywhere. The screaming has mostly stopped.
Sovereign's Tongue
EXPERTISE 2 MIND CONTROL DR 16
Implants a simple command in the target's mind. Nothing complex—"forget you saw me," "unlock that door," "walk away." The Man's servants are particularly vulnerable to this, being already conditioned to follow orders without question.
MY METHODOLOGY: I inscribe the command in golden ink on the inside of my left palm before encountering the target. During conversation, I make eye contact, press my palm to my forehead in an apparently casual gesture, and recite an innocuous phrase in conversation that contains the command encoded in gematria. Their subconscious picks it up even as their conscious mind ignores it.
⚙ Mechanics
Component Value Notes
Required Arts Pneuma +2 To control mind
Base DR 14 Standard difficulty
Resistance +2 DR Target may test Presence to resist
Final DR 16 Against unwilling target
Duration d6 minutes Or until command fulfilled
Complexity Simple only One action, clearly defined
Components: Golden ink, direct eye contact, encoded phrase
Backlash Risk: HIGH. Mental backlash can reverse the command onto yourself.
⚠ Ethical Note:
I use this sparingly. Not out of morality—I'm beyond the Man's pathetic "ethics"—but because enslaving minds attracts attention from Things that also enjoy that particular vice. You don't want to end up on a demon's radar as competition.
Prestidigitator's Veil
EXPERTISE 2 SLEIGHT OF HAND DR 14
Enhanced manual dexterity combined with perceptual misdirection. Perfect for stealing exotic components, planting tracking sigils, or swapping genuine reality fragments with fakes when dealing with untrustworthy buyers.
MY METHODOLOGY: I anoint my hands with an oil I've prepared from moonflower and mercury (don't ask about the mercury fumes—I have Concerns about my long-term health). While my hands are still slick, I practice the motion I'll need to execute exactly 33 times. On the 33rd repetition, the muscle memory becomes magically perfect.
⚙ Mechanics
Component Value Notes
Required Arts Pneuma +1, Vita +1 Mind + body enhancement
Base DR 14 Standard difficulty
Additional Art +2 DR For using Vita
Final DR 16 But -2 if in Sanctuary with prep
Duration One action Single perfect motion
Components: Moonflower oil, mercury, 33 practice repetitions
Backlash Risk: Moderate. Failed castings have caused temporary paralysis of hands.
Smoke Step
EXPERTISE 2 ESCAPE DR 16
Transform my body temporarily into smoke, allowing me to slip through cracks, under doors, through ventilation systems. The ultimate escape mechanism when collecting Reality Scars in surveilled corporate facilities.
MY METHODOLOGY: I must prepare by fasting for 24 hours (to make my body "lighter"). I inhale incense smoke—specifically a blend I make from sage, tobacco, and a particular fungus found only at Reality Scars—while sitting in a circle of salt. As I exhale, I visualize my solid form dispersing like cigarette smoke, and speak the formula of dissolution.
⚙ Mechanics
Component Value Notes
Required Arts Alchemy +2, Vita +2 Transform matter + living flesh
Base DR 14 Standard difficulty
Additional Art +2 DR For second Art (Vita)
Final DR 16 Self-transformation
Duration d6 minutes Or until I choose to solidify
Vulnerability Strong wind Disperses me—painful reassembly
Components: Reality Scar fungus, sage, tobacco, salt circle, 24-hour fast
Backlash Risk: EXTREME. I once failed to fully resolidify for a week. Still finding smoke in odd places.
⚠ Critical Warning:
Do NOT use this spell if it's windy. Do NOT use this spell if there are fans running. Do NOT sneeze while incorporeal. These lessons were learned the hard way.

/// Level Three: Master Workings ///
We have reached the apex of my current understanding. These spells require absolute precision, extensive preparation, and significant sacrifice. I reserve them for dire circumstances or when the reward—a Reality Scar fragment, for instance—justifies the risk.
The Burning Sovereign
EXPERTISE 3 PYR - COMBAT DR 20
Summon a controlled inferno shaped to my will. I can direct it as a lance of flame, a protective wall, or—in one memorable incident—a flaming sword that actually functioned. This is my go-to when dealing with the Things that guard Reality Scars.
MY METHODOLOGY: Fire is chaos constrained. I begin by constructing a geometric pattern of flammable materials—usually alcohol-soaked rope in the shape of sacred geometry. I light it while chanting the Promethean invocation, then "grab" the fire with my will and reshape it. The key is maintaining the geometric structure even as the fire moves—imagine sculpting with something that wants desperately to burn you.
⚙ Mechanics
Component Value Notes
Required Arts Pyr +3 To control fire/heat/energy
Base DR 14 Standard magical difficulty
Power Scale +6 DR For significant destructive force
Final DR 20 Master-level working
Damage 2d6 To d4 targets in close grouping
Duration d6 rounds Requires concentration
Prep Time d6 hours Minimum, with components
Components: Geometric rope pattern, alcohol, source of flame, uninterrupted space
Cost: I usually sacrifice d3 HP to reduce DR
Backlash Risk: MAXIMUM. Fumbles have left me with 2nd-degree burns and once set my Sanctuary on fire.
⚠ Danger Assessment: EXTREME
This spell has saved my life four times and nearly killed me six times. The math is not in its favor. Only use when absolutely necessary. Keep a fire extinguisher handy. Know where the nearest burn ward is located.
Gates of Horn and Ivory
EXPERTISE 3 TRAVEL - EMERGENCY DR 22
Open a portal to my Sanctuary from anywhere. My ultimate escape hatch. When everything goes wrong—and it will—this spell has pulled me out of collapsed buildings, demon-infested warehouses, and once, from inside a malfunctioning teleportation experiment that was rapidly approaching critical mass.
MY METHODOLOGY: This requires permanent preparation in the Sanctuary itself. I've carved two doorframes—one made of genuine horn (expensive!), one of ivory (even more expensive!)—and positioned them as a pair. The horn gate leads TO my sanctuary, the ivory gate leads FROM it. When I need extraction, I draw a doorframe in the air with a piece of blessed chalk while speaking the words of return. The air becomes a door, and I step through.
⚙ Mechanics
Component Value Notes
Required Arts Chora +3, Chronos +2 Space + Time manipulation
Permanent Setup DR 30 One-time creation in Sanctuary
Activation DR 20 To open from remote location
Additional Art +2 DR For using Chronos
Final Activation 22 Emergency use
Range Unlimited Tied to Sanctuary itself
Duration d6 seconds Must move fast
Permanent Components: Horn doorframe, ivory doorframe, extensive ritual work
Activation Components: Blessed chalk, words of return (must be memorized)
Cost: The initial setup cost me significant Wealth and d6 HP in sacrificial blood to bind it
Backlash Risk: CATASTROPHIC if fumbled. Could collapse the portal while you're inside it.
⚠ Setup Warning:
The initial creation of this spell took me three months of preparation and nearly bankrupted me. The horn and ivory must be ethically sourced (spiritually speaking—murdered animals leave taint). I used mammoth ivory and ram's horn blessed by three different traditions. Worth every penny.
Crown of the Sovereign Mind
EXPERTISE 3 MIND CONTROL - ADVANCED DR 24
Complete mental domination of a single individual. Not mere suggestion—total control. I can see through their eyes, speak through their mouth, control their actions. I've used this exactly three times, and each time I felt a piece of my humanity crack. But when you need information from a corporate security chief about Reality Scar locations, squeamishness is a luxury.
MY METHODOLOGY: I require something of the target—hair, blood, or (best) a photograph taken without their knowledge. I place this in a silver bowl of mercury and stare into it while it spins, matching my breathing to the rhythm of the spiral. When the spiral pattern reaches exactly 13 rotations, I speak their true name (which I must discover beforehand) and step into their mind.
⚙ Mechanics
Component Value Notes
Required Arts Pneuma +3 Master-level mental magic
Base DR 14 Standard difficulty
Full Control +6 DR Complete domination vs suggestion
Resistance +4 DR Target tests Presence to resist
Final DR 24 Against unwilling, aware target
Duration d6 hours Or until I release them
Range Unlimited If I have proper components
Components: Target's true name, personal item from target (hair/blood/unknowing photo), silver bowl, mercury, uninterrupted concentration
Cost: Usually sacrifice d6 HP to reduce DR to manageable levels
Backlash Risk: SOUL-DESTROYING. A fumble could trap your consciousness in their body while they take yours.
⚠ Psychological Warning:
This spell damages the caster psychologically. When you wear another person's mind, you bring some of it back with you. I still have memories that aren't mine. I still sometimes prefer my coffee the way the security chief did. I dream his dreams. Use this spell only when there is no alternative.
Personal Note: I nearly didn't include this spell in the grimoire. It violates something fundamental. But I've learned that Reality Scars are guarded by things far worse than ethical quandaries. Sometimes you must become the monster to hunt the monsters. Just... try not to enjoy it.

/// Reality Scar Operations ///
These workings are specifically designed for locating, accessing, and harvesting materials from Reality Scars. They represent my life's work and the reason I've survived where others haven't.
The Frayed Edge Detection
EXPERTISE 2 DETECTION DR 16
Sense the presence of Reality Scars within a wide radius. The world becomes... translucent. I can see where consensus reality has worn thin, where the Man's control falters. Appears as a kind of visual static, like dead pixels in reality itself.
MY METHODOLOGY: I've created special lenses from glass harvested at a Reality Scar (the glass formed when lightning struck sand at a dimensional breach—perfect sympathetic link). I mount these in wire frames and wear them while burning a candle made from beeswax mixed with ground Reality Scar fragments. The combination lets me literally see the tears.
⚙ Mechanics
Component Value Notes
Required Arts Pneuma +2, Chora +1 Perception + Space
Base DR 14 Standard difficulty
Additional Art +2 DR For Chora
Exotic Component -2 DR Reality Scar glass lenses
Exotic Component -2 DR Reality Scar fragment candle
Final DR 12 With proper preparation
Range 1 mile radius Stronger scars visible further
Duration d6 hours While candle burns
Components: Reality Scar glass lenses, candle with ground Reality Scar wax
Backlash Risk: Moderate. Can cause temporary blindness or perception of things that aren't there. Or worse—things that ARE there but shouldn't be visible.
Harvester's Protection
EXPERTISE 3 PROTECTION DR 20
Creates a protective barrier against the... inhabitants... of Reality Scars. Things That Never Were hate this spell, which is exactly why I developed it. The barrier doesn't make me invisible to them, but it does prevent them from easily tearing me apart when I'm collecting Quantum Spaghetti or other materials.
MY METHODOLOGY: I draw a circle around myself using salt that has been blessed in three different religious traditions (ecumenical approach—can't be too careful). I place four mirrors at the cardinal points, angled to reflect any approaching entity back at itself. Then I recite the formula of sovereignty while visualizing an invisible shell around me. The mirrors provide the reflective barrier, the salt provides the binding.
⚙ Mechanics
Component Value Notes
Required Arts Pneuma +3, Alchemy +2 Spirit ward + physical barrier
Base DR 14 Standard difficulty
Additional Art +2 DR For Alchemy
Power Level +4 DR Protection vs Bestiary-level entities
Final DR 20 Master working
Duration d6 hours Or until circle broken
Mobility None Must stay in circle—limitation
Components: Triple-blessed salt, four mirrors (silver-backed), formula of sovereignty
Cost: Usually sacrifice d4 HP to strengthen the barrier
Backlash Risk: HIGH. A fumbled protection spell can actually ATTRACT entities rather than repel them.
⚠ Usage Note:
This spell makes me stationary. I use it as a "safe room" while cataloging Reality Scar findings, not during active collection. Combine with Smoke Step or Gates of Horn for emergency exit strategy.

/// Final Notes ///
This grimoire represents years of work, numerous failures, and more than a few close calls with death, madness, and things worse than either. I share this knowledge not out of generosity—we High Ritualists are not known for that—but because the work is bigger than any one practitioner.

Reality Scars are multiplying. The Man's control is fracturing. Whether this heralds liberation or apocalypse remains to be seen. Either way, someone needs to study these phenomena, harvest their materials, and perhaps—just perhaps—find a way to either seal them or tear reality wide open.

I haven't decided which goal I prefer yet.

Use this knowledge wisely. Or don't. Wisdom is overrated when you're trying to overthrow consensus reality.

Invoke often.

— Fjord the Hermit
