Fjord's Walking Staff

Artifact · Magic Item
⚠ EXOTIC COMPONENT SOURCE ⚠

/// DESCRIPTION

Crafted from a gnarled, silent branch, once the very limb of a soul transformed into timber by forgotten magic, Fjord's Walking Staff hums with a quiet, persistent energy. Its surface, smooth in some places and roughly textured in others, seems to remember ancient whispers and the slow, deliberate pulse of a tree's life. Embedded near its tip, almost unseen, are shimmering threads of what can only be described as Quantum Spaghetti, faintly glowing with the captured essence of paradox and possibility.
◆◆◆
⚡ THE SCAVENGER'S COMPASS ⚡
EFFECT: Once per day, the wielder may activate The Scavenger's Compass, guiding them unerringly to the nearest Reality Scar without need for a roll. The staff vibrates gently in the hand, its unique composition attuned to the subtle tears in the fabric of existence, drawing the bearer towards places where the veil between worlds thins.
NO ROLL REQUIRED: This is a prepared magical effect bound into the staff itself. The wielder simply activates the compass function through force of will (Presence DR 12 to activate a held spell).

/// EXOTIC COMPONENTS

THE BRANCH: (-4 DR)
Once the very limb of a soul transformed into timber by forgotten magic. This is not just wood—it's the physical manifestation of a transformation curse, retaining the memory of what it once was. The wood "remembers" being alive, being human, being changed. This sympathetic connection to transformation makes it ideal for binding complex magical effects.
QUANTUM SPAGHETTI: (-4 DR)
Shimmering threads embedded near the tip, faintly glowing with the captured essence of paradox and possibility. This is reality itself in its most unstable form—strands of probability and causality made quasi-physical. Only obtainable at Reality Scars where the laws of physics break down. This component provides an intrinsic connection to the very phenomena the staff is designed to detect.

/// GM NOTES

WHAT ARE REALITY SCARS?
Places where the veil between worlds thins. Locations where the Man's control over consensus reality has fractured. Sites of catastrophic magical failures, dimensional breaches, or where the Laws of Science break down entirely. These are dangerous places—the kind where you might find Quantum Spaghetti, encounter Things That Never Were, or stumble into Nowhere.

STORY HOOKS:
• Why does Fjord need to find Reality Scars?
• What happens when the staff leads you somewhere you don't want to go?
• Can the Man track the staff's activation?
• What else might be drawn to Reality Scars?
